**Key Gap:** 70% of studies carry no phase designation, and only ~5% reach Phase 3/4. This indicates a **massive bottleneck in late-stage, high-evidence trials** — prosthetic devices are not advancing through the clinical pipeline efficiently. Most activity is at the feasibility/observational stage.

### Enrollment Trends by Sponsor Type

| Sponsor Type | Count | % of Total | Interpretation |
|-------------|------:|-----------:|----------------|
| OTHER (Academic/University) | 313 | 73.3% | Dominant; mostly single-site academic studies |
| INDUSTRY (Commercial) | 59 | 13.8% | Underrepresented — limited commercial device testing |
| FED (Federal/Government) | 41 | 9.6% | VA/DoD-funded research |
| OTHER_GOV | 9 | 2.1% | State/local government |
| NIH | 2 | 0.5% | Virtually no NIH-funded prosthetic trials |
| NETWORK | 2 | 0.5% | Multi-site collaborative trials |
| AMBIG | 1 | 0.2% | Unclear sponsor type |

**Key Gaps Identified:**

1. **Industry underrepresentation (13.8%):** Only 59 of 427 trials are industry-sponsored. This means the commercial prosthetic market is not investing adequately in clinical evidence generation — a critical bottleneck for device innovation and FDA clearance.

2. **NIH near-absence (0.5%):** Only 2 NIH-funded prosthetic trials exist. Major federal health research funding is not flowing into this area despite the large amputee population.

3. **Academic dominance (73.3%):** Single-site university studies cannot scale. Only 2 NETWORK-type trials suggest multi-site collaboration is rare.

4. **Underserved populations:** The heavy academic/federal skew means trials are concentrated at major teaching hospitals. Rural amputees, veterans in non-VA facilities, and lower-income communities have **severely limited trial access**.

## 3. Proposed Outreach Strategies

### Strategy 1: Target the Phase Gap — Late-Stage Trial Awareness
**Problem:** 70% of prosthetic trials are unphased/early feasibility; almost no Phase 3/4 trials exist.  
**Strategy:** Partner with Shirley Ryan AbilityLab's research office to:
- Create a **"Trial Ready" education program** helping prosthetic users understand Phase 3/4 trial participation
- Develop plain-language materials explaining why late-stage trials matter for device access
- Recruit prosthetic users as **trial ambassadors** who can share their experiences

### Strategy 2: Bridge the Industry Gap
**Problem:** Only 14% of trials are industry-sponsored; limited commercial device testing.  
**Strategy:**
- Facilitate **industry-academia matchmaking** at the Shirley Ryan AbilityLab site
- Host quarterly **"Prosthetic Innovation Roundtables"** connecting device companies with potential trial participants
- Advocate for NIH funding increases specifically for prosthetic rehabilitation trials

### Strategy 3: Address Underserved Populations
**Problem:** Academic/federal trials concentrate at major centers; rural, veteran, and low-income amputees are excluded.  
**Strategy:**
- Deploy a **mobile outreach unit** (paired with the site visit) to suburban VA clinics and rural rehabilitation centers
- Create **telehealth-enabled trial screening** to expand geographic reach
- Partner with **Illinois limb loss support groups** (especially in Downstate IL and rural areas)
- Ensure materials are available in **Spanish** given Chicago's large Hispanic/Latino population

### Strategy 4: Improve Site Accessibility for Outreach
**Problem:** No pharmacy within 500m; height-restricted parking; limited transit for wheelchair users.  
**Strategy:**
- **Coordinate with Northwestern Memorial Hospital** for an on-site pharmacy kiosk or medication pickup during outreach events
- **Reserve accessible parking** at the Sheraton Grand underground entrance (verify van clearance first)
- Establish a **dedicated shuttle pickup point** at the Divvy station at McClurg Ct & Ohio St
- Create **wheelchair-accessible event materials** with clear entrance directions avoiding ramps/elevators where possible
- Schedule outreach events around **CTA bus routes** on Erie Street for those who can navigate bus transfers

### Strategy 5: Leverage Existing Capabilities
**Opportunity:** Shirley Ryan AbilityLab itself sponsored a low-cost prosthetic socket trial (NCT04725461) specifically for developing countries.  
**Strategy:**
- Highlight this trial as a **model for global outreach** and replicate the model locally
- Use the lab's existing rehabilitation infrastructure for **on-site prosthetic fitting demos** during outreach
- Train outreach staff using the lab's **prosthetic rehabilitation expertise**
